Did we find the game?
Yes — and on time.
The tilt (the timecard that clocks feelings) became the engine: every beat after it pays the same unusual thing forward instead of inventing a new one.
Heighten, or just add?
Heightened.
"Two hours of mild dread" → "overtime on a panic attack" is the same joke raised a floor, not a new absurdity stacked on top. That's UCB heightening, not a topping contest.
Any question that stalled the platform?
One — caught it.
"What is this place?" early on put the burden back on the partner. The Actor rescued it by answering with an offer. Cleaner: make the statement yourself.
Next time, try

Skip the one question. When you feel the urge to ask "where are we?", tell us instead — name the place and hand your partner a gift. You'll reach the platform a full beat sooner and the tilt will hit harder.

? About this demo — what's real vs. illustrative
What this is A game-film replay of one improv scene, annotated like a director's prompt-book. The hero artifact is the replay itself.
Real The transport works for real — Play, Step, and the Scrubber all drive the same hand-authored timeline; notes fire at stored timestamps; the see-saw, tilt-circle, and Harold card are live UI.
Illustrative / hand-authored The 12-line scene and every director note are written by hand as an exemplar of good craft. Nothing here was generated by a model at runtime — that's the honest nature of a replay.
To go live "Live mode" (disabled) would let you actually improvise against the model. That requires an Anthropic API key and a small serverless proxy (Vercel function / Cloudflare Worker) so the key never ships to the browser, plus a director-model prompt that scores status / platform / tilt / game turn-by-turn. See the README.
Theory grounding Keith Johnstone — status, platform, the tilt, "make your partner look good." UCB — base reality, the first unusual thing, the game of the scene.
